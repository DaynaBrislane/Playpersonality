How to work with Grace

Energy and Communication

Do

Lead with warmth before business — they connect through relationship first, so a little personal check-in goes a long way.

Avoid

Cold or transactional communication. Jumping straight to the task without acknowledgement can feel dismissive to them.

Tip

Make them feel valued before diving into feedback — they're far more open to new ideas when they feel respected and included.

Information Processing

Do

Give them concrete, practical information with real examples — they process best when ideas are grounded in the real world rather than abstract concepts.

Avoid

Overloading them with too many hypotheticals or big-picture thinking without context. Ambiguity can feel unsettling rather than exciting.

Tip

Connect new information to something familiar or already working — they're much more receptive when change feels like an evolution, not a reinvention.

Decision Making

Do

Present options with clear pros and cons — they appreciate a structured framework to evaluate rather than open-ended brainstorming.

Avoid

Pushing for quick decisions without giving them time to consider the impact on people involved. They need space to weigh the human side.

Tip

Acknowledge the team impact of any decision before diving into logistics — they'll commit faster when they feel the people side is covered.

Organization

Do

Provide clear timelines and expectations upfront — they thrive with structure and appreciate knowing exactly what's needed and when.

Avoid

Last-minute changes to plans or scope — shifting priorities without context can feel chaotic and undermine their ability to deliver their best work.

Tip

Use shared checklists or project boards — they love visible progress and feel most confident when the plan is documented and trackable.
